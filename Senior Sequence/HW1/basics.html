What You Will Need

  1. A pan

    2. Seriously just a pan. If all you have is one, like I did, or if it's scratched up on the bottom... I mean it's not recommended but we all need to eat so get what you can. Also for the longest time I was just building recipes from the bottom up in one large pan because I just didn't own much else and I didn't want a lot of cleanup. Results are better when you cook your base in dish and your sauce in another but don't let this be a barrier.

  2. Measuring cups

    3. Look there's no way around this and you can by a crappy set at dollar tree so just get em. (Bonus: They can double as tiny bowls or giant spoons)

  3. Some sort of stirring device

    4. Spoon, spatula, etc. Don't use a fork or a knife (done it) because it's ineffective and damaging to the pan. Respect the pan.

  4. A flat surface for chopping

    5. Often I'm using a plate because I'm lazy but a cutting board is recommended

  5. A knife

    6. Use a sharp one and I would NOT recommend the serated steak knifes from dollar tree... but I have been known to use those.

  6. Salt and pepper

    7. You'll notice I didn't include any instructions for how much seasoning to put in your sauces. It's still a good idea to use but I don't know your life so you put as much or as little as you want.

Some basics I always have on hand
